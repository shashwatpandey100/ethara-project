================================================================================
  SECTION 1 — INTRODUCTION  (30 seconds)
================================================================================

  "Ethara is an AI-powered team task manager built for engineering teams.
   It combines classic project management — projects, tasks, kanban boards —
   with AI voice standups that automatically transcribe your daily standup
   and convert spoken updates into actual tasks in your board.

   Let me walk you through the full product end to end."

  SECTION 3 — DASHBOARD  (30 seconds)
================================================================================

  Navigate to: /dashboard

  "The dashboard gives you a quick summary — your total projects,
   task counts across statuses, and recent activity.
   Everything updates in real time as your team makes changes."

  Point out:
  - Stats cards at the top (total projects, tasks by status)
  - Recent activity feed

================================================================================
  SECTION 4 — PROJECTS  (1.5 minutes)
================================================================================

  Navigate to: /dashboard/projects

  "Here's the projects list. Notice the table layout —
   each row shows the project name, description, task count,
   member count, and how long ago it was created."

  STEP 1 — Search
  ────────────────
  Type something in the search bar.

  "Search is debounced — it fires 300ms after you stop typing,
   no full page reload, no submit button needed.
   Pagination sits right next to search, only appears when there's more
   than one page of results."

  STEP 2 — Create a new project
  ──────────────────────────────
  Click "New Project".

  Fill in:
    Name:        Demo Sprint
    Description: Live demo project for Ethara

  Click Create.

  "Project is created instantly. You're automatically the admin."

  STEP 3 — Open the project
  ──────────────────────────
  Click on "Demo Sprint" in the table.

  "This is the project view. Three tabs at the top:
   Tasks, Standups, and Members.
   Let's start with tasks."

================================================================================
  SECTION 5 — TASKS & KANBAN BOARD  (2 minutes)
================================================================================

  Navigate to the Tasks tab.

  "The kanban board has four columns — To Do, In Progress, In Review, Done.
   Columns grow vertically as you add tasks, they don't overflow or clip.
   Let me add a few tasks to show the board in action."

  STEP 1 — Add Task 1
  ────────────────────
  Click "Add Task" (top right).

  Fill in:
    Title:       Design login page
    Description: Create wireframes and final UI for the login screen
    Priority:    High
    Due date:    (pick any date)

  Click Create Task.

  STEP 2 — Add Task 2
  ────────────────────
  Click "Add Task" again.

  Fill in:
    Title:    Set up database schema
    Priority: Urgent

  Click Create Task.

  STEP 3 — Add Task 3
  ────────────────────
  Fill in:
    Title:    Write API documentation
    Priority: Low

  Click Create Task.

  "Now I have three tasks in To Do. Let me move one."

  STEP 4 — Drag and drop
  ───────────────────────
  Drag "Design login page" from To Do → In Progress.

  "Tasks can be dragged between columns.
   The status updates immediately in the database."

  STEP 5 — Open a task
  ─────────────────────
  Click on "Set up database schema".

  "The task detail dialog shows all the metadata —
   priority badge, status selector, assignee, due date.
   There's a comments thread at the bottom for inline discussion.
   Let me add a comment."

  Type a comment: "Schema drafted, pending review."
  Click send.

  Close the dialog.

  STEP 6 — Bulk delete
  ─────────────────────
  Check the checkbox on "Write API documentation".
  Check "Design login page".

  "When you select tasks a delete bar appears at the top.
   Useful for cleaning up a sprint at end of cycle."

  Click Delete Selected → confirm.

================================================================================
  SECTION 6 — MEMBERS  (45 seconds)
================================================================================

  Click the Members tab.

  "Members panel shows everyone on the project with their role.
   As admin I can invite new members via an invite link."

  Click Invite.

  "This generates a shareable join link.
   Anyone with the link can join the project directly —
   no manual approval needed, no email invite flow to set up."

  Copy the link. Show it.

  "In a real team scenario a second user would open this link,
   sign up or log in, and instantly land in this project."

  Close the invite dialog.

================================================================================
  SECTION 7 — VOICE STANDUPS  (3 minutes — the highlight)
================================================================================

  Click the Standups tab.

  "This is the feature that makes Ethara different.
   Instead of typing your daily standup into Slack or a ticket,
   you just record it — like you'd say it in a real standup meeting.
   The AI handles the rest."

  STEP 1 — Open the recording dialog
  ────────────────────────────────────
  Click "Record Standup" (top right of the history section).

  "A dialog opens. One big microphone button.
   Click it to start."

  STEP 2 — Record
  ────────────────
  Click the mic button. Start recording. Say something like:

  --- SPEAK THIS ---
  "Hey team, quick standup update.
   Yesterday I finished the login page design and pushed it for review.
   Today I'm working on setting up the database schema —
   need to get the user and sessions tables in place.
   One blocker: I'm waiting on the API keys from the infrastructure team,
   so I can't test the auth flow until those come in."
  --- END ---

  Point at the waveform while recording:
  "You can see the live waveform — the bars react to your voice in real time."

  STEP 3 — Stop
  ──────────────
  Click the stop button (red square).

  "Recording stops. An audio preview appears so you can
   listen back before submitting."

  Play the preview briefly.

  STEP 4 — Add title and submit
  ──────────────────────────────
  In the title field type: Day 1 Standup

  Click "Submit Standup".

  "Ethara uploads the audio to cloud storage, sends it to Deepgram for
   transcription, then passes the transcript to Google Gemini for analysis.
   The standup card appears in the history immediately with a 'processing'
   status. Let me refresh in a moment."

  Wait 15–30 seconds, then refresh or watch the status update.

  STEP 5 — Show the result
  ─────────────────────────
  Click on the standup row to expand it.

  "The AI generates a structured summary — four sections:
   what was accomplished, what's in progress, any blockers, key decisions.
   Clean formatting, easy to skim.

   Below that — this is the powerful part — the AI has extracted
   actionable tasks from what I said."

  Point at the extracted tasks:
  - "Push login page for review"
  - "Set up database schema"
  - "Waiting on API keys — blocker"

  "Each one has a priority and a suggested assignee based on what was said.
   I can check the ones I want to create and click Create Selected."

  STEP 6 — Create tasks from standup
  ────────────────────────────────────
  Check all extracted tasks.
  Click "Create Selected".

  "Those tasks are now on the kanban board.
   No copy-pasting, no manual ticket creation.
   One voice recording turns into a full set of tracked tasks."

  Switch to Tasks tab to show the new tasks appeared.

================================================================================
  SECTION 8 — AI DIGEST  (30 seconds)
================================================================================

  Switch back to the project header (Tasks tab).
  Click "AI Digest" button (top right, admin only).

  "The AI Digest gives a project-level summary on demand.
   It looks at all open tasks, their statuses and priorities,
   and generates a paragraph summary of where the project stands."

  Wait a moment for the digest to generate. Read it out.

  "Useful for a quick async update to stakeholders —
   no status meeting needed."

  "The settings page covers three areas.
   Profile — update your display name.
   Password — change password with current password confirmation.
   Session — sign out of this device or sign out everywhere."

  Point at each section briefly.

  SECTION 10 — CLOSING  (30 seconds)

  "So that's Ethara end to end.

   Projects and kanban boards are the foundation.
   Members and roles keep access clean.
   And the voice standup feature closes the loop —
   your daily standup becomes your task list automatically.

   The stack is Next.js 15 on the frontend, Express with Better Auth
   on the backend, Drizzle ORM against PostgreSQL on Supabase,
   Deepgram for transcription, and Gemini for AI summaries.
